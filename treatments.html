Lawn Care

"What are Carbon Treatments for lawn care?" While traditional fertilizers feed the grass, carbon treatments are designed to feed the soil. In lawn care, a carbon treatment involves applying high-carbon materials—like biochar, humic acid, or specialized organic compost—to the turf. The goal is to build up the "soil organic matter," which acts as the biological engine for a healthy lawn. Think of carbon as the infrastructure of your soil. Without it, chemical fertilizers often just wash away or become "locked" in the soil where roots can't reach them.

"Why are Carbon Treatments Important?" Carbon treatments are becoming the "holy grail" of modern lawn care because they fix the one thing that traditional fertilizers can't: the soil’s ability to actually hold onto and use nutrients. If you think of fertilizer as the "fuel" for your grass, carbon is the "gas tank." Without a high-capacity tank, most of your expensive fertilizer just leaks out or evaporates.

What is Grub Control? Grub control is the practice of managing the population of "white grubs"—the larval stage of various beetles (such as Japanese Beetles, June Bugs, and European Chafers)—to prevent them from destroying your lawn. Grubs live underground and feed exclusively on the roots of your grass. Because they eat the "anchor" and the nutrient delivery system of the plant, a heavy infestation can kill large sections of a lawn in a very short amount of time.

Why is Grub Control Important? Grub control is often considered the "insurance policy" of a professional lawn care program. Because grubs live underground and are invisible until the damage is done, a sudden infestation can be one of the most disheartening and expensive problems a homeowner can face.
